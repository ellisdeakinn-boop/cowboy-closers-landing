The Real Problem

It's not the script.

It's the frame behind it.

Here's what actually happens on a call when it falls apart. You needed the person to say yes. You didn't say that out loud — you didn't have to. It was in the pace you spoke at when they pushed back. In the over-explanation when they went quiet. In the way you kept talking when you should have shut up and let the silence sit.

The prospect felt it — they always feel it — and the moment they did, they became the authority in the room. No script saves you from that point. The cleanest objection reframe ever written won't hold if the energy behind it is needy. They'll find a way out every time.

The unlock is genuine detachment from the outcome. Not as a technique. Actually meaning it — in the room, in real time, when there's money on the table. That's the frame that makes every other skill you build actually land.
