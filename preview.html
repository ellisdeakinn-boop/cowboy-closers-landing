The Man Behind It

10 years.
No bullsh*t.

I figured out you could make money by talking to people when I was about ten. My old man was a magician. He'd pull two kids on stage, do a little game, and the winner would get a glowstick. Then he'd say, "if anyone else wants one, it's £2 a piece, with little Connor down the back." People would run over with cash. Boom, boom, boom. That was the first time it clicked.

Spent my teens hustling. Balloon modelling at kids' parties on weekends, construction whenever sales was thin, water sports instructor in Greece in the summers, barman in France in the winters, cutting hair in the basement on the side. Always selling something.

Saved up at 23 and moved to Mexico to do timeshare. Selling £80-100k worth of packages a month pretty quickly and thought I'd worked the whole thing out. Spoiler: I hadn't. Blew through it. Ended up in Bali, lost money on an agency, scaled landing pages from two grand to thirty in three weeks, then went all in on remote closing.

Stopped drinking three years ago and that's when everything I'd been chasing finally started landing. The unlock was never another job or another country. It was building a brand around the skill I already had. 200 million views last year. 120k followers. Hit my first £50k profit month in 2025.

Cowboy Closers came out of all of it. Same skill, just taught the way it actually works on a live call when someone's looking for a reason to walk.

The Real Problem

It's not the script.
It's the frame behind it.

The problem sitting behind every closer who's grinding without results is the frame they bring to a call. Whether the person on the other side can feel, even slightly, that they need the deal. And they always can feel it. It might be subtle. A slight rush when you're pushing toward the close, over-explaining when someone goes quiet, keeping talking when you should just shut up and let the silence sit.

But it's there. And the moment they pick it up, you've handed the authority in that conversation straight over to them. No script saves you from that. You can have the cleanest objection reframe ever written and they'll still find a way out. Every time.

The fix is genuine detachment from the outcome. Not as a technique you layer on top of your existing approach. Actually having it. In the room, in real time, when there's money on the table. That's what the program builds first. Everything else is built on top of that.
