The Man Behind It

10 years.
No bullsh*t.

I figured out you could make money by talking to people when I was about ten. My old man was a magician. He'd pull two kids on stage, do a little game, and the winner would get a glowstick. Then he'd say, "if anyone else wants one, it's £2 a piece, with little Connor down the back." People would run over with cash. Boom, boom, boom. That was the first time it clicked.

Saved up at 23 and moved to Mexico to do timeshare. Selling £80-100k worth of packages a month pretty quickly and thought I'd worked the whole thing out. Spoiler: I hadn't. Blew through it. Ended up in Bali, lost money on an agency, scaled a landing page business from two grand to thirty in three weeks, then went all in on remote closing.

Stopped drinking three years ago and that's when everything I'd been chasing finally started landing. The unlock was never another job or another country. It was building a brand around the skill I already had.
