The Real Problem

It's not the script.

It's the frame behind it.

The problem sitting behind every closer who's grinding without results is the frame they bring to a call. Whether the person on the other side can feel — even slightly — that they need the deal. And they always can feel it. It might be subtle: a slight rush when you're pushing toward the close, over-explaining when someone goes quiet, keeping talking when you should just shut up and let the silence sit.

But it's there. And the moment they pick it up, you've handed the authority in that conversation straight over to them. No script saves you from that. You can have the cleanest objection reframe ever written and they'll still find a way out. Every time.

The fix is genuine detachment from the outcome. Not as a technique you layer on top of your existing approach. Actually having it — in the room, in real time, when there's money on the table. That's what the program builds first. Everything else is built on top of that.
